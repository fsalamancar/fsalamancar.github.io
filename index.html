i.The approach

Medicine should be written for one person — not averaged across millions.

I'm Fran, a biologist from the National University of Colombia and an MSc student in Bioinformatics. My focus is precision medicine of the gut: integrating multi-omics — the host genome, the transcriptome, the intestinal microbiome and the metabolome — to understand the gut barrier and the host–microbiome dialogue that shapes health.

Precision medicine isn't a slogan to me — it's the discipline of reading enough molecular layers, closely enough, that the answer becomes specific to a single body.

ii.What I work on

Stacking the omics of the gut into one clinical decision.

01 — Genomics

Host variants

Calling, annotating and prioritising the host variants that shape gut function and disease risk.

02 — Transcriptomics

What the gut expresses

Bulk and single-cell RNA — reading the molecular state of the intestinal epithelium and its barrier.

03 — Microbiome

Metagenomics

Profiling the gut ecosystem — who is there, what they carry, and how they talk to the host.

04 — Metabolomics

The chemical output

The small molecules where host and microbiome meet — the readout of what the gut is actually doing.

05 — Integration

Multi-omics pipelines

Reproducible workflows that fuse the omic layers into one coherent, interpretable picture.

06 — Translation

Clinical meaning

Connecting molecular layers to phenotype — the bridge between gut data and a patient's reality.
