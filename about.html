About

Biologist turned
bioinformatician.

i.Who I am

I'm Fran, a biologist from the National University of Colombia and an MSc student in Bioinformatics who found his passion where life meets data — specifically, in the gut.

My focus is precision medicine of the gut: integrating multi-omics — host genomics, transcriptomics, the intestinal microbiome and metabolomics — to understand the gut barrier and the host–microbiome dialogue. My background in the natural sciences keeps me grounded in biology, while code and computation let me read those molecular layers together and make the data specific to a single person.

ii.What drives me
01

Curious by nature

Whether in a dataset or on a forest trail, I'm constantly exploring — finding patterns in data, in genomes, in the microbial world of the gut. My fascination with how host and microbiome shape each other feeds my drive to understand health from both a molecular and a systems-level perspective.

Explore
02

Sharing what I learn

Science shouldn't be kept in a silo. I'm passionate about making knowledge accessible — through tutorials, tools and insights from my projects. I enjoy teaching, mentoring and building things that help others understand the complexity and beauty of biology through data.

Teach

On the edge of discovery

From gene regulation to coding pipelines — and from travelling the world to building reproducible workflows — I thrive at the intersection of curiosity and innovation, staying on the cutting edge of science and technology to uncover the hidden layers of how life works.
